The high mobility group (HMG) transcription factor TCF-1 is essential for early T cell development. Using gain- and loss-of-function experiments, we discovered that the co-occupancy of TCF-1 and the architectural protein CTCF altered the structure of topologically associating domains in T cell progenitors, leading to interactions between previously insulated regulatory elements and target genes at late stages of T cell development. The TCF-1-dependent gain in long-range interactions was linked to deposition of the active enhancer mark H3K27ac and recruitment of transcriptional machinery.
