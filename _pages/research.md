Our lab uses **single-cell epigenomics** to study how gene regulation in brain and immune cells contributes to Alzheimer's Disease. We combine cutting-edge genomics technologies with computational analyses to generate mechanistic insights into AD pathogenesis.

---

## Research Areas

### 1. Single-Cell Epigenomics of Alzheimer's Disease

Alzheimer's Disease involves complex changes in gene regulation across multiple cell types in the brain. We perform **single-cell ATAC-seq (scATAC-seq)** and related assays on post-mortem human brain tissue spanning different stages of AD progression. Our goal is to build comprehensive maps of chromatin accessibility changes across cell types and disease states.

**Key questions:**
- Which cis-regulatory elements are dysregulated in AD-vulnerable cell types?
- How does chromatin remodeling differ between early and late stages of AD?
- Can epigenetic signatures predict disease progression?

---

### 2. Neuroimmune Interactions in Alzheimer's Disease

Microglia, the brain-resident immune cells, and peripheral immune cells play critical roles in AD. We profile **microglia** and **infiltrating immune cells** using single-cell multi-omics to understand how neuroinflammation is epigenetically regulated.

**Key questions:**
- What epigenetic programs drive microglial activation states in AD?
- How do peripheral immune cells (e.g., T cells, monocytes) contribute to AD neuroinflammation?
- Can modulating immune cell epigenomes provide therapeutic benefit?

---

### 3. Multi-Omic Integration and Spatial Transcriptomics

We integrate **scRNA-seq**, **scATAC-seq**, and **spatial transcriptomics** data to build a systems-level view of AD pathology. By linking regulatory elements to gene expression changes and placing them in spatial context within brain tissue, we aim to identify disease-relevant cell states and pathways.

**Key questions:**
- How do transcriptional and epigenetic changes co-vary across single cells in AD?
- What is the spatial organization of dysregulated cell states in the AD brain?
- Which transcription factors drive cell-type-specific gene regulatory networks in AD?

---

### 4. Computational Tool Development

We develop and apply **computational methods** for analyzing single-cell epigenomic data, including tools for peak calling, dimensionality reduction, cell-type deconvolution, and regulatory network inference.

**Key projects:**
- Benchmarking and improving scATAC-seq analysis pipelines
- Reference atlas construction for brain cell epigenomes
- Machine learning approaches for disease variant interpretation

## Technologies We Use

| Technology | Application |
|---|---|
| scATAC-seq | Single-cell chromatin accessibility |
| scRNA-seq | Single-cell gene expression |
| CUT&TAG / CUT&RUN | Histone modifications & TF binding |
| Spatial transcriptomics | Spatial gene expression mapping |
| snMultiome | Paired RNA+ATAC from single nuclei |
| Bulk ATAC-seq / ChIP-seq | Population-level epigenomics |
